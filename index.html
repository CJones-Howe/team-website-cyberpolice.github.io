What is Stuxnet?

Stuxnet is a computer worm that first made headlines in 2010. It used zero-day Windows vulnerabilities and searched infected PCs for a connection to the software that controlled Iranian centrifuges. It then used this connection to send instructions that damaged the equipment.

Timeline of Events

2005-2007: U.S. and Israel begin planning to disrupt Iranian nuclear facilities (Operation Olympic Games).
2009: Stuxnet is implemented and released.
2010: Malware is discovered by cybersecurity companies.
2011: The public becomes aware of the operation.
Today: Stuxnet is considered one of the most well-known cyberattacks in history.
